SDG 11: Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable

Major NGOs

C40

Cities is a network of the world's megacities committed to addressing
climate change. It is taking bold climate action and leading the way towards having a more sustainable future.
C40 also offers cities an effective forum to collaborate, share knowledge and drive meaningful action on climate change.
A group of significant cities worldwide that have made a commitment to combating climate change is known as the C40 network.
The primary objective is to lower greenhouse gas emissions while simultaneously promoting their economies and growth in ways
that respect the environment and the welfare of people. Through the partnership, experts and mayors may collaborate to take climate action,
incorporating the community, the commercial sector, and other governmental levels. To lessen the consequences of climate change, effective teamwork,
knowledge sharing, and the encouragement of meaningful, quantifiable activities are required.

ICLEI

More than 2,500 local and regional governments worldwide are members of the international network ICLEI -
Local Governments for Sustainability, which is dedicated to sustainable urban development. We advocate for low-emission, environmentally friendly, just, resilient, and circular development locally and have an impact on sustainability policies throughout more than 125 countries where we operate.
Currently serving ICLEI Members in Indonesia, Malaysia, the Philippines, and Thailand is the ICLEI Southeast Asia Secretariat (ICLEI SEAS),
which is headquartered in Manila, Philippines. When ICLEI SEAS was founded in 1999, it started out in the area
with the Cities for Climate Protection (CCP) campaign in the Philippines. In 2002, it moved
on to Thailand and Indonesia. Since then, ICLEI's membership in these three nations has expanded quickly, prompting the
creation of the Indonesia Office in 2013 and the inclusion of Malaysia in 2014.

Rockerfeller Foundation

In order to assist more communities in becoming resilient to the increasing number of social, economic,
and physical difficulties that the 21st century brings with it, The Rockefeller Foundation launched the 100 Resilient communities initiative
in 2013. The 100RC network's cities have been equipped with the tools required to create a resilience
roadmap along four primary pathways: financial and operational support for the creation of a novel new role in the local
government—the Chief Resilience Officer—who will oversee the resilience initiatives of the city; professional assistance in creating
a strong resilience strategy; access to resources, partners, and service providers from the public, commercial, and non-profit sectors
who can assist them in creating and putting into practice their resilience strategies; and membership in an international
