Featured Programs

Metro Manila Flood Management Project

The Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH) and the Metro Manila Development Authority (MMDA) collaborated on the Metro Manila Flood Management Project, which was approved in September 2017 and began to be implemented in the first quarter of 2018. The project was supported by the World Bank and Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank. The Metro Manila Flood Management Project aims to enhance flood control in specific regions of Metro Manila. The project will reduce the amount of solid waste discharged in rivers, create 20 new pumping stations, update the 36 that are currently in use, and provide housing for impacted individuals and communities. By constructing dependable infrastructure to lessen losses from floods, the project would contribute to making Metro Manila more resilient.

Toxic Substances and Hazardous Waste Management

This DENR-EMB program addresses the safe and appropriate management of industrial chemicals and hazardous wastes in all phases of their life cycle, including importation, manufacture, distribution, transportation, use, and disposal, in accordance with RA 6969 Toxic Substances and Hazardous and Nuclear Waste Control Act of 1990. By significantly reducing their release into the air, water, and soil, this program will help achieve target 12.4: the environmentally sound management of chemicals and all wastes throughout their life cycle, in compliance with established international frameworks, to minimize their detrimental effects on human health and the environment.

Resettlement Program

The National Housing Authority (NHA) is responsible for housing production through the Resettlement Program (RP), which involves the acquisition and development of large tracts of raw land to create serviced lots and housing units for families displaced from government infrastructure projects and danger areas. The NHA targets Informal Settler Families (ISFs) through different modalities, such as completed housing resettlement projects, home material loan projects, and joint venture schemes with Local Government Units (LGUs). The RP can involve completed housing or incremental housing strategies. A recent initiative is the 50 billion Oplan Likas housing program, which aims to relocate around 104,000 ISFs residing in danger areas in Metro Manila.

UN Habitat

UN Habitat focuses on sustainable development in cities and communities in order to solve urban challenges. Its goal is to ensure inclusivity, minimize environmental impact while working on improving housing, infrastructure, transportation and green spaces. The organization promotes disaster-resilient urban planning and social equity as well as climate change adaptation with the aim of building safe, resource-efficient and resilient cities.

Clean Air Asia

Clean Air Asia ensures clean air and sustainable growth for all cities in Asia which is its prime aim. This group cooperates with governments, businesses and people in order to better manage air quality, reduce pollution levels and consider the effects of bad air on health.

DHSUD

The Philippines' Department of Human Settlements and Urban Development (DHSUD) concentrates on the advancement of housing, planning and development in cities with an eye towards establishing sustainable, inclusive and resilient communities. By promoting better living conditions, managing urban growth and tackling concerns such as affordability of housing units or infrastructure development, DHSUD tries hard to make life easier for everyone in the country.
