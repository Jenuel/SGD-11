1960s - 1970s

Early Concerns and Foundations

The rapid urbanization of the post-World War II era led to significant concerns about the growth of slums, inadequate infrastructure, and environmental degradation in cities. The concept of sustainable development began to take shape, with early reports and conferences addressing urban challenges, including the 1976 Vancouver Conference on Human Settlements.

1980s

Early Concerns and Foundations

The Brundtland Commission's 1987 report, "Our Common Future," introduced the concept of sustainable development and highlighted the need for balanced growth that does not compromise the ability of future generations to meet their needs. The idea of sustainable urban development began to gain traction.

1990s

Global Initiatives and the Millenium Development Goals

The United Nations began to focus more directly on urban issues, culminating in the Habitat II conference in 1996, which resulted in the Istanbul Declaration and the Habitat Agenda. These documents outlined strategies for improving the living conditions in urban areas and laid the groundwork for future sustainable urban development initiatives.

2000s

Global Initiatives and Millenium Development Goals

The Millennium Development Goals (MDGs), established in 2000, included targets related to improving living conditions in slums, though they did not specifically focus on urban sustainability as comprehensively as SDG 11 would later.

2012

Formation of the Sustainable Development Goals

The United Nations Conference on Sustainable Development (Rio+20) set the stage for the post-2015 development agenda. Discussions during this conference emphasized the need for a new, comprehensive framework to address urbanization, leading to the formulation of SDGs.

2015

Formation of the Sustainable Development Goals

The 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development was adopted by all United Nations Member States during the UN Sustainable Development Summit in September 2015. SDG 11 was officially included as one of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals. This goal was designed to address the specific challenges of urbanization, such as ensuring safe and affordable housing, sustainable transport, and resilience to disasters.

2016-Present

Early Concerns and Foundations

Since the adoption of SDG 11, efforts have been directed at integrating this goal into national policies, urban planning strategies, and international cooperation. The goal has been incorporated into various local and global initiatives aimed at improving urban resilience, enhancing infrastructure, and promoting sustainable urban environments.

Future

Early Concerns and Foundations

By 2025, significant advancements in smart city technologies are expected, including enhanced data analytics and improved integration of the Internet of Things (IoT) to better urban living conditions. By 2030, the target year for achieving the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), substantial progress is anticipated in reducing urban inequality, improving public transportation, and increasing green spaces. Beyond 2030, efforts will continue to maintain and build upon the progress made, with a particular focus on adapting to emerging challenges such as climate change and population growth.
