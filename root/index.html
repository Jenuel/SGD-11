ABOUT US

We are undergraduate college students of Computer Science who are dedicated to supporting sustainable development through the United Nations’ Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). These 17 goals, established in 2015, aim to address important global challenges such as poverty, inequality, climate change, and access to justice by 2030. The SDGs provide a clear framework for governments, businesses, and communities to work together toward a more sustainable and fair future.

Our approach emphasizes the interconnected nature of these goals (SDGs), encouraging collaboration across different sectors to solve local and global challenges. We believe that by aligning our efforts with the SDGs, we can support positive change and contribute to a more sustainable, balanced world. Our work on promoting practical and achievable solutions that contribute to better urban planning, environmental care, and social fairness. We aim to raise awareness, encourage cooperation, and support efforts that help create a more sustainable future for everyone.

OUR MISSION

Our mission is to raise awareness about building cities that are inclusive, safe, and sustainable. As more people move to cities 70% of the world’s population is expected to live in urban areas by 2050 we believe that informed people and communities can help solve the challenges of rapid growth. Today, over a billion people live in poor housing, and many cities struggle to provide the infrastructure and services needed.

As college students, our mission is about learning and sharing information. By spreading awareness and discussing how cities are planned and managed, students can make a difference in their own communities. Small actions, like talking about the need for better housing, public transport, and green spaces, can lead to real change. We want to help students understand the importance of planning for safer, healthier, and more resilient cities. Through sharing ideas and raising awareness, students can be part of building a future where cities are better for everyone.

ABOUT SDG 11

The Growing Urban Shift

The globe is getting more and more urbanized. Since 2007, almost half of all people on Earth have lived in cities, and by 2030, that number is expected to increase to 60%.

Balancing Economic Growth and Environmental Sustainability in Cities

Cities and metropolitan regions, which account for around 60% of the world's GDP, are engines of economic expansion. But they also contribute more than 60% of resource use and around 70% of global carbon emissions.

Addressing Urbanization's Impact on Slums and Infrastructure

An increasing number of people live in slums as a result of rapid urbanization, which is also aggravating air pollution, causing unplanned urban sprawl, and overloading infrastructure and services such as garbage collection, water and sanitation systems, roads, and transportation).

Mitigating COVID-19's Effects on Vulnerable Urban Populations

Poor, densely populated metropolitan areas, especially where a billion people live in slums and informal settlements, will be severely impacted by COVID-19. High population density makes it difficult to follow social distancing and self-isolation guidelines.

Ensuring Food Security for Vulnerable Urban Communities

Without steps to guarantee that the impoverished and vulnerable citizens have access to food, the UN food agency, FAO, issued a warning that hunger and mortality rates might sharply increase in metropolitan areas.
