SDG 11: Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable

WHAT IS SDG?

The Sustainable Development GoalsSDGs are a set of 17 global objectives established by the United Nations in 2015. They aim to address a wide range of issues, including poverty, inequality, climate change, environmental degradation, peace, and justice, by 2030. The SDGs provide a universal framework for governments, businesses, and communities to work together towards a more sustainable and equitable future for all.

Each of the 17 SDGs is interconnected, emphasizing the need for an integrated approach to achieve sustainable development. The goals are designed to be inclusive, leaving no one behind, and they encourage collaboration across sectors, countries, and organizations. By aligning efforts with the SDGs, entities can contribute to global progress while also addressing local challenges, ultimately fostering a healthier planet and more prosperous societies. As the world faces increasing environmental and social pressures, the SDGs serve as a critical roadmap for guiding policy, business strategies, and community initiatives toward a resilient and sustainable future.

ABOUT SDG 11

The Growing Urban Shift

The globe is getting more and more urbanized. Since 2007, almost half of all people on Earth have lived in cities, and by 2030, that number is expected to increase to 60%.

Balancing Economic Growth and Environmental Sustainability in Cities

Cities and metropolitan regions, which account for around 60% of the world's GDP, are engines of economic expansion. But they also contribute more than 60% of resource use and around 70% of global carbon emissions.

Addressing Urbanization's Impact on Slums and Infrastructure

An increasing number of people live in slums as a result of rapid urbanization, which is also aggravating air pollution, causing unplanned urban sprawl, and overloading infrastructure and servicessuch as garbage collection, water and sanitation systems, roads, and transportation).

Mitigating COVID-19's Effects on Vulnerable Urban Populations

Poor and highly populated metropolitan regions will be particularly hard hit by COVID-19's effects, especially for the billion people living in slums and informal settlements all over the world, where population density further complicates the implementation of advised practices like social distance and self-isolation.

Ensuring Food Security for Vulnerable Urban Communities

Without steps to guarantee that the impoverished and vulnerable citizens have access to food, the UN food agency, FAO, issued a warning that hunger and mortality rates might sharply increase in metropolitan areas.
